<p><p>The biosimilars treatment market consists of three main types: recombinant non-glycosylated proteins, recombinant glycosylated proteins, and recombinant peptides. Recombinant non-glycosylated proteins lack sugar chains and are often simpler in structure, commonly used for therapeutic applications. Recombinant glycosylated proteins have added sugar components, enhancing their functionality and effectiveness in mimicking natural proteins. Recombinant peptides are shorter chains of amino acids, offering targeted therapeutic options for various diseases. Together, these categories help diversify treatment options and increase accessibility in the healthcare market.</p></p>
