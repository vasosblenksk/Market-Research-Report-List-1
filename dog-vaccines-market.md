<p><ul><li>Attenuated Live Vaccines</li><li>Conjugate Vaccines</li><li>Inactivated Vaccines</li><li>Subunit Vaccines</li><li>Toxoid Vaccines</li><li>DNA Vaccines</li><li>Recombinant Vaccines</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The dog vaccines market includes various types tailored to enhance canine health. Attenuated live vaccines use weakened pathogens to stimulate immunity. Inactivated vaccines employ killed pathogens for safety. Conjugate vaccines combine polysaccharides with proteins to boost immune response. Subunit vaccines utilize specific antigen fragments, while toxoid vaccines neutralize toxins. DNA vaccines introduce genetic material to provoke an immune response, and recombinant vaccines use engineered organisms to deliver antigens, providing targeted protection against diseases. Each type serves distinct purposes in canine health management.</p></p>
