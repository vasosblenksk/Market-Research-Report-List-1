<p><p>The trans fatty acids market comprises two main types: natural and synthetic trans fatty acids. Natural trans fatty acids are found in small quantities in certain animal products, like dairy and meat, produced through biological processes. In contrast, synthetic trans fatty acids are industrially created through hydrogenation, a process that transforms liquid oils into solid fats, commonly used in processed foods for stability and shelf life. Both types are subject to health concerns, leading to regulatory measures and declining usage.</p></p>
