<p><p>3D bioprinting in life science R&D involves various innovative techniques. Magnetic 3D bioprinting uses magnetic fields to organize and position cells, enhancing structural complexity. Laser-assisted bioprinting employs lasers to precisely deposit biomaterials, enabling high resolution and accuracy. Inkjet 3D bioprinting utilizes thermal or piezoelectric technology to eject droplets of bioinks, offering rapid and scalable fabrication. Microextrusion 3D bioprinting involves pushing viscous bioinks through fine nozzles, facilitating the creation of larger, tissue-like structures with excellent mechanical properties.</p></p>
