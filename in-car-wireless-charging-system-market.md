<p><ul><li>Electromagnetic Induction</li><li>Magnetic Resonance</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The in-car wireless charging system market comprises two primary types: electromagnetic induction and magnetic resonance. Electromagnetic induction uses coils to transfer energy through a magnetic field, requiring close contact between the charging pad and the device. Magnetic resonance, on the other hand, allows for charging at a greater distance, using resonant coupling to transfer power more efficiently and enabling multiple devices to be charged simultaneously. Both technologies aim to enhance convenience and usability for electric vehicle and mobile device users.</p></p>
