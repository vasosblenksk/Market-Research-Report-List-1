<p><p>Weft knitted fabrics are categorized into various types based on their construction techniques and patterns. Double knit fabrics feature two sets of yarns, providing thickness and durability, ideal for activewear and outerwear. Circular knit fabrics are produced in a continuous loop, resulting in a seamless finish, commonly used for t-shirts and hosiery. Fair Isle knit involves intricate color patterns typical in sweaters, celebrated for its aesthetic appeal. Cable knit presents a textured, intertwined pattern, often used in cozy garments like sweaters and scarves.</p></p>
