<p><p>The composite bonded magnet market primarily encompasses two molding types: injection molding and pressing molding. Injection molding involves melting the magnet material and injecting it into a mold, allowing for intricate designs and high-volume production. In contrast, pressing molding utilizes a powder mixture of magnetic materials that is compacted and heated to form the final product, offering better material density and strength. Both methods cater to diverse applications in industries like automotive, electronics, and renewable energy, driven by performance and efficiency demands.</p></p>
